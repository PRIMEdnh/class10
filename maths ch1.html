Short Answer Questions (NCERT)

  1. Prove that √3 is irrational.
    Answer

    Assume √3 is rational. Then √3 = p/q (in lowest terms). Squaring both sides: 3 = p²/q² → p² = 3q². So p² is divisible by 3 → p divisible by 3 → p = 3k. Substituting back: (3k)² = 3q² → 9k² = 3q² → q² = 3k² → q divisible by 3. Both p and q divisible by 3 → contradicts lowest terms → Hence √3 is irrational.

  2. Factorise x² - 5x + 6.
    Answer x² - 5x + 6 = (x - 2)(x - 3)
  3. Use Euclid's division lemma to show that the square of any positive integer is either of the form 3m or 3m + 1.
    Answer Any integer n can be written as 3q, 3q+1, or 3q+2. Squaring: (3q)² = 9q² = 3(3q²) → of form 3m (3q+1)² = 9q²+6q+1 = 3(3q²+2q) + 1 → of form 3m+1 (3q+2)² = 9q²+12q+4 = 3(3q²+4q+1) + 1 → of form 3m+1 Hence proved.
  4. Factorise x² + 5x + 6.
    Answer x² + 5x + 6 = (x + 2)(x + 3)

Long Answer Questions (NCERT)

  1. Prove that √2 is irrational.
    Answer

    Assume √2 is rational → √2 = p/q in lowest terms. Squaring: 2 = p²/q² → p² = 2q². So p is even → p = 2k. Then 4k² = 2q² → q² = 2k² → q is even. Both divisible by 2 → contradiction → √2 is irrational.

  2. Find HCF and LCM of 36, 60 and 84 using prime factorization.
    Answer 36 = 2² × 3², 60 = 2² × 3 × 5, 84 = 2² × 3 × 7 HCF = 2² × 3 = 12, LCM = 2² × 3² × 5 × 7 = 1260
  3. Find the HCF of 210, 45 and 105 using prime factorization.
    Answer 210 = 2 × 3 × 5 × 7, 45 = 3² × 5, 105 = 3 × 5 × 7 → HCF = 3 × 5 = 15
  4. Factorise x² + 7x + 12.
    Answer x² + 7x + 12 = (x + 3)(x + 4)
  5. Factorise x² - 8x + 15.
    Answer x² - 8x + 15 = (x - 3)(x - 5)
  6. Prove that the square of any odd number is of the form 4q + 1.
    Answer Odd number = 2n + 1 → Square = (2n + 1)² = 4n² + 4n + 1 = 4q + 1 (q = n(n+1))
  7. Find HCF and LCM of 45 and 75 using Euclid’s division lemma.
    Answer 75 ÷ 45 = 1 remainder 30 → 45 ÷ 30 = 1 remainder 15 → 30 ÷ 15 = 2 remainder 0 → HCF = 15 LCM = (45 × 75)/15 = 225
  8. Check if 101 is a prime number.
    Answer 101 has no divisors other than 1 and 101 → prime
  9. Express 180 and 150 as product of primes and find HCF.
    Answer 180 = 2² × 3² × 5, 150 = 2 × 3 × 5² → HCF = 2 × 3 × 5 = 30
  10. Find the HCF of 84, 126, 210, 330 using prime factorization.
    Answer 84 = 2² × 3 × 7, 126 = 2 × 3² × 7, 210 = 2 × 3 × 5 × 7, 330 = 2 × 3 × 5 × 11 → HCF = 2 × 3 = 6
