Very Short Answer Questions (NCERT)

  1. What is a linear equation in two variables?
    AnswerAn equation of the form ax + by + c = 0, where a, b ‚â† 0, is called a linear equation in two variables.
  2. Give an example of a linear equation in two variables.
    Answer2x + 3y = 6
  3. What is the graphical method?
    AnswerIt is a method to find the solution of a system of equations by plotting lines on a graph.
  4. State the solution of 2x + y = 5 and x - y = 1.
    Answerx = 2, y = 1
  5. What do two intersecting lines represent?
    AnswerA unique solution of the system of equations.
  6. Two parallel lines represent what type of system?
    AnswerNo solution.
  7. Two coincident lines represent what type of system?
    AnswerInfinite solutions.
  8. What is the slope of the line 2x + 3y = 6?
    Answer-2/3
  9. State one method to solve a system of linear equations.
    AnswerGraphical method / Substitution / Elimination
  10. What is the x-intercept of 3x + 2y = 6?
    Answer2
  11. What is the y-intercept of 3x + 2y = 6?
    Answer3
  12. Give an example of a system with no solution.
    Answer2x + 3y = 6 and 4x + 6y = 10
  13. Give an example of a system with infinite solutions.
    Answer2x + 3y = 6 and 4x + 6y = 12
  14. State the condition for a system to have a unique solution.
    AnswerLines intersect at one point (slopes are different).
  15. State the condition for a system to have no solution.
    AnswerLines are parallel (slopes are equal, different intercepts).
